Autonomous Driving Simulation
Gaussian Splatting for 3D Scene Reconstruction

Project Overview

This project explores integrating Gaussian Splatting into an autonomous driving simulation workflow for photorealistic 3D scene reconstruction and fast novel-view rendering. The goal is to enable rapid environment playback and perception testing by reconstructing driving scenes from multi-view image sequences and then rendering them in real time for planning and control evaluation.

Pipeline

  1. Data Capture: Collect multi-view images and camera poses from a simulator or dataset.
  2. 3D Reconstruction: Train a Gaussian Splatting model to represent the scene.
  3. Fast Rendering: Render novel views for ego camera and surround cameras at interactive rates.
  4. Simulation Integration: Feed rendered frames to perception and planning modules for closed-loop tests.
